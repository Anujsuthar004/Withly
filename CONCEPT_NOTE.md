# Tag Along - App Concept Note

## 1) Product Vision
Tag Along is a trust-first companionship platform for two high-value moments:
- Social plus-one: users who want company for events, parties, public gatherings, and city experiences.
- Errand companionship: users who want a reliable companion for practical tasks (hospital visits, paperwork runs, shopping, travel transfers, and unfamiliar locations).

The moat is category breadth with one safety layer: most products do either social discovery or task support, not both under one verified companionship identity.

## 2) Problem Statement
People frequently face two similar emotional and practical frictions:
- "I want to go, but I do not want to go alone."
- "I need to go, but I want support or safety while doing it."

Current alternatives are fragmented:
- Friends are not always available.
- Generic social apps are slow/noisy for immediate needs.
- Task platforms optimize for labor, not companionship and trust.

## 3) Core Users
- Students/new-city professionals looking for event company.
- People with social anxiety or low-confidence in unfamiliar spaces.
- Users needing support for errands where company improves comfort or safety.
- Shift workers and travelers requiring companion presence for specific windows.

## 4) Value Proposition
- Fast match for "go now" social plans.
- Structured companionship for errands with clarity on expectations.
- Unified trust system across both use cases (verification, reputation, check-ins).

## 5) Product Scope (MVP)
### A) Modes
- Social Mode: event + hangout categories.
- Errand Mode: practical companionship categories.

### B) Request Post
- Title, category, location, start time, duration, vibe/intent tags.
- Optional boundaries: conversation level, budget split, transport preference.
- Safety preferences: verified-only, same-language preference, check-in enabled.

### C) Match & Discovery
- Nearby requests and available companions feed.
- Matching score based on distance, mode fit, shared tags, and reliability score.
- Quick accept flow plus short pre-meet chat prompts.

### D) Safety Layer (non-negotiable)
- Account verification (email/phone + optional government ID).
- Trust badges (verified identity, repeat reliability, no-show rate).
- Live check-ins during active sessions.
- Emergency contact + SOS shortcut.
- Report, block, and moderation queue.

## 6) Matching Principles
- Hard constraints: location radius, availability overlap, blocked users, verification filters.
- Soft ranking: shared interests, category history, response speed, completion score.
- Bias control: avoid over-concentrating visibility to only top-rated users by adding fair exposure slots.

## 7) Business & Monetization (Early)
- Freemium posting and joining.
- Premium: priority placement, advanced filters, boosted safety features.
- B2B (later): campuses, events, and communities with closed-group trust networks.

## 8) Risks & Mitigations
- Safety incidents: strict moderation SLA, proactive check-ins, risk scoring.
- Misaligned expectations: structured request templates and clear boundaries.
- No-shows: reliability scoring, temporary penalties, deposits for certain categories.
- Category confusion: explicit Social vs Errand UX entry points.

## 9) Rollout Strategy
### Phase 1 (0-2 months)
- One city pilot.
- Two social categories + two errand categories.
- Concierge moderation and manual trust checks.

### Phase 2 (2-6 months)
- Automated moderation tooling.
- Reputation model improvements.
- Community partnerships (colleges, coworking groups).

### Phase 3 (6+ months)
- Multi-city expansion.
- Team plans for organizations/events.
- Personalized companion recommendations.

## 10) Success Metrics
- Match time (median time-to-first-accept).
- Request completion rate.
- Repeat user rate (30-day retention).
- Incident rate per 1,000 sessions.
- Trust score growth and no-show reduction.

## 11) MVP Build Decision
Build web MVP focused on:
- category clarity (Social + Errand)
- quick posting and matching simulation
- trust indicators and safety interactions

This allows user interviews and concierge validation before backend-heavy scaling.
